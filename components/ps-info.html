All About Power Supplies

The power supply unit (PSU) is a crucial component that provides electrical power to your entire PC. It converts the power from your wall outlet into a form that your computer components can use.

Main Functions of a Power Supply

  • Converts AC power from the wall outlet to DC power used by the computer components.
  • Regulates and distributes power to the motherboard, CPU, GPU, drives, and other components.
  • Provides stable and reliable power to ensure smooth and efficient operation.

Types of Power Supplies

  • Non-Modular: All cables are permanently attached to the PSU. Generally more affordable, but less flexible for cable management.
  • Modular: Allows you to connect only the cables you need, reducing clutter and improving airflow.
  • Semi-Modular: A mix of permanently attached and detachable cables. Offers a balance between cost and cable management.

Key Features to Consider

  • Wattage: Ensure the PSU provides enough power for all your components, with some headroom for future upgrades.
  • Efficiency Rating: Look for PSUs with an 80 Plus certification (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, or Titanium) for better efficiency and lower energy costs.
  • Cable Management: Modular or semi-modular PSUs can help with keeping your build tidy.
  • Form Factor: Ensure the PSU fits your case (ATX, SFX, etc.).
  • Connectors: Make sure the PSU has all the necessary connectors for your components, including PCIe for GPUs and SATA for drives.
